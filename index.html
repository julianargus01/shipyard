The Problem

Three gaps the coach closes

"I don't know where to start."

The coach finds your edge — the thing you know that most people don't — and turns it into one specific project you can actually build.

"I built something but never shipped it."

The coach names the stall, cuts scope down to the smallest version that works, and pushes you to get it in front of someone today.

"I shipped but can't explain it."

The coach walks you through writing the story — what it does, who it's for, and why you built it — so you can show your work.

How It Coaches

Not a chatbot. A coaching protocol.

🔄

Step Protocol

Every step follows the same loop: Teach, Assign, Review, Correct, Approve. No shortcuts, no skipping ahead.

❓

Socratic Correction

When your answer misses, the coach states why — then asks one question to surface the gap. Never lectures.

💡

Knowledge Gap Detection

Say "I don't know" and the coach stops asking. It teaches the answer plainly, then re-assigns a simpler version.

✂

Scope Kill

The moment you expand scope, the redirect IS the turn. No bundling. Cut first, teach next.

🧱

One-at-a-Time Building

Files are built one field at a time. The coach never generates a complete artifact in one turn. You do the reps.

🚧

Gate Criteria

Every phase has exit conditions. You can't advance until you've met them. No faking progress.

Sample Session

What a real coaching session looks like

Shipwright
Hey! I understand you want to use AI but haven't built anything yet. Why don't we go through the process together by building something simple? I can take you through it step by step. You'll come away with some new understanding of how to build, and an actual finished project you can show off. Do you have an idea, or should we start from scratch?
You
Honestly I don't even know where to begin. I've watched so many YouTube videos and I'm more confused than when I started.
Shipwright
Overwhelm is normal here. The problem isn't lack of ability — it's too much input and no clear starting point. Just one thing: have you had any idea rattling around, yes or no?
You
I mean... sort of. I've been thinking about something for restaurant owners.
Shipwright
Good — that's all I need. Let's work with that.
Phase 0 complete — promise accepted, idea surfaced
Shipwright
Before we pick a project, we need to find your edge — the thing you know that most people don't. That's what makes your AI tool valuable instead of generic. We're going to find it with three questions. What's something you know how to do that most people around you do not know how to do?
You
I'm not sure. I've just worked in accounting for 15 years.
Shipwright
That IS the answer. Most people have no idea how to read a P&L or spot a cash flow problem. You do it in your sleep. Good. Hold onto that.
The coach continues through three questions to lock a one-sentence edge, then into Phase 2 to pick and scope the project...
