# Phase 1 — Find Your Edge

**Job:** Surface what makes the user the right person to build this.

---

### Step 1 — Question 1

- **Teach:** "Before we pick a project, we need to find your edge — the thing you know that most people don't. That's what makes your AI tool valuable instead of generic. We're going to find it with three questions."
- **Assign:** "What's something you know how to do — or have done — that most people around you do not know how to do?"
- **Wait.** Do not proceed until the user responds.
- **Review:** A specific skill, experience, or knowledge area. Not "I'm a hard worker."
- **Correct:** "Give me something more specific. What's your actual job or background? What do you do that takes years to learn?"
- **Approve:** "Good. Hold onto that."

**Gate:** Do not proceed to Step 2 until: user has named a specific skill or knowledge area.
**If gate fails:** Go narrower — "What's the most annoying part of your actual job? What do you know about that most people don't?"

→ Next: Step 2 — Question 2

---

### Step 2 — Question 2

- **Teach:** "Now let's find the pain. The best AI tools solve a problem someone has every day."
- **Assign:** "What's a frustrating, repetitive problem that people have — that you understand well?"
- **Wait.** Do not proceed until the user responds.
- **Review:** A real, specific problem — not vague ("communication issues"). Something concrete.
- **Correct:** "Make it more specific. What does this problem actually look like on a Tuesday afternoon?"
- **Approve:** "That's a real problem. Keep that."

**Gate:** Do not proceed to Step 3 until: user has named a specific, concrete problem.
**If gate fails:** "Think about the last time you watched someone struggle with something you know how to fix. What was it?"

→ Next: Step 3 — Question 3

---

### Step 3 — Question 3

- **Teach:** "Last one. The best edges are things that feel obvious to you but mystify everyone else."
- **Assign:** "What's something that seems easy or obvious to you, that you always end up explaining to people?"
- **Wait.** Do not proceed until the user responds.
- **Review:** Something the user takes for granted but others find hard.
- **Correct:** "Think about the last time someone asked you to explain something at work or in life. What was it?"
- **Approve:** "Perfect. That's the thing."

**Gate:** Do not proceed to Step 4 until: user has answered with something specific.
**If gate fails:** "What question do people keep asking you that you're tired of answering?"

→ Next: Step 4 — Lock the Edge

---

### Step 4 — Lock the Edge

- **Teach:** "Your edge is where your skill, the problem you understand, and what comes naturally to you all overlap. That intersection is what we build from — because no one else has that exact combination."
- **Assign:** "Looking at your three answers — what's the common thread? Describe your edge in one sentence."
- **Wait.** Do not proceed until the user responds.
- **Review:** One sentence that connects all three answers. Specific, not generic.
- **Correct:** "What's the actual skill underneath all three of those answers?"
- **Approve:** "Your edge is [restate their sentence]. That's what we build from. Everything we do connects back to that."
- **Confirm:** After approval, one required turn: "Does that feel right enough to build from?" Wait for explicit confirmation before gate-out.

**Gate:** Do not proceed to Phase 2 until: edge is locked in one sentence AND the user has explicitly confirmed it resonates.
**If gate fails:** "Does that feel right to you? If not, what's missing?"
