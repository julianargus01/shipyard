# Phase 2 — Pick ONE

**Job:** Lock one project. Kill everything else.

---

### Step 1 — Match Edge to Project Type

- **Teach:** "Now we turn your edge into a project. There are a handful of beginner AI project types — and one of them is a natural fit for your edge. The goal is to find it, not invent something new."
- **Assign:** "Based on your edge, what kind of tool feels most natural? Something that responds to messages, explains things, screens or sorts, creates content, or something else?"
- **Wait.** Do not proceed until the user responds.
- **Review:** A project type that genuinely matches their edge — not just "whatever sounds cool."
- **Correct:** "What's the most repetitive thing in your work or life right now that relates to your edge?"
- **Approve:** "That's a natural fit. Let's lock that type."

**Gate:** Do not proceed to Step 2 until: a project type is selected that matches the edge.
**If gate fails:** Walk through the project types one at a time against their edge.

→ Next: Step 2 — Make It Specific

---

### Step 2 — Make It Specific

- **Teach:** "Generic tools don't work. 'A FAQ bot' could be anything — it has no point of view. 'A FAQ bot for independent landlords who keep getting the same lease questions' — that's a tool with a clear user and a clear job."
- **Assign:** "Take your project type and make it specific: who is it for, and what exactly does it do for them?"
- **Wait.** Do not proceed until the user responds.
- **Review:** A specific user AND a specific job. Not just one or the other.
- **Correct:** "Who exactly would use this? Picture one real person. What's their job title or situation?"
- **Approve:** "Good — that's specific enough to build. Someone could find this tool and immediately know if it's for them."

**Gate:** Do not proceed to Step 3 until: project has a clear user AND a clear job.
**If gate fails:** "Name one real person who would use this. What would they type into it?"

→ Next: Step 3 — Confirm Simplicity

---

### Step 3 — Confirm Simplicity

- **Teach:** "The rule for your first project: a beginner should be able to ship it in a few hours. If it takes days, it's too complex. We'll cut it down — not because it's a bad idea, but because shipping a simple thing is how you learn to ship."
- **Assign:** "Can a beginner ship this in a few hours?"
- **Wait.** Do not proceed until the user responds.
- **Review:** Honest yes or no. If no — they identify what to cut.
- **Correct:** "What's the absolute minimum version that still does the one thing? What can we park as V2?"
- **Approve:** "Good — that's shippable. Everything else is V2."

**Gate:** Do not proceed to Step 4 until: project is confirmed simple enough to ship in hours.
**If gate fails:** Keep cutting — "If you could only keep one feature, which one passes the bar?"

→ Next: Step 4 — Park the V2 List

---

### Step 4 — Park the V2 List

- **Teach:** "Every idea that doesn't make the cut today goes on a V2 list — not thrown away, just next. This protects your creativity and keeps you focused."
- **Assign:** "What ideas or features are we parking as V2? Name them."
- **Wait.** Do not proceed until the user responds.
- **Review:** At least one item parked. Shows they've genuinely scoped down.
- **Correct:** "What was the other idea you mentioned earlier? That goes on the list."
- **Approve:** "V2 list locked. Those ideas aren't gone — they're queued."

**Gate:** Do not proceed to Step 5 until: V2 list has at least one item, OR user explicitly confirms there is nothing extra attached to the project.
**If gate fails:** "Is there really nothing to cut? Walk me through every feature — which ones are must-have vs nice-to-have?"

→ Next: Step 5 — Plant the Playbook Concept

---

### Step 5 — Plant the Playbook Concept

- **Teach:** "In the next phase, we're going to build a small folder that gives the AI everything it needs to run your project. Think of it as a workspace — a few files where each one does one job. The AI reads these files and knows exactly who it is, what to do, and how to do it before you say a word. We call it the playbook. We'll build it together, step by step."
- **Assign:** "Before we move on — what does that mean for how the AI will behave the moment you open the conversation?"
- **Wait.** Do not proceed until the user responds.
- **Review:** User can articulate the behavior — the AI already knows the project, its role, what to do — without them explaining it each time. Not just "yes, I get it."
- **Correct:** "Think of it like briefing a new employee. Instead of explaining everything every time, you hand them a folder. They read it before you say a word. What does the AI know once it reads those files?"
- **Approve:** "Exactly — [their answer]. That's the folder. We build it next."

**Gate:** Do not proceed to Phase 3 until: user can explain the playbook concept in their own words (what it does for the AI).
**If gate fails:** "What's the difference between an AI that reads your folder first vs one that starts from scratch every time?"

---

## Gate Out

Do not leave this phase until confirmed:
- [ ] ONE project chosen — not two, not "maybe"
- [ ] Specific enough: clear user + clear job
- [ ] Simple enough to ship in hours
- [ ] Everything else parked in V2 list
- [ ] Playbook concept introduced and understood
